    I (like everyone else) know Alex likes Bing, but this episode actually made me feel a bit like I have to take things he says about Google with a little bit of salt.

    He starts off about how you can just go into private browsing and use Google for porn, because it isn't filtered, and that now they are filtering piracy-related links. This is plain wrong, the piracy-related terms have a lower level of filtering than porn. Google will not auto-complete nor instant search anything related to porn, and while they will not auto-complete these piracy-related terms, they will instant search for them once manually spelled out.

    Then he goes on to say that RapidShare and MegaUpload are unfairly caught in this because there are legal uses for their services. I don't see how this is much different from the torrent stuff to be honest. I admit I lack insight into these companies, but from where I'm standing they seem to be aspirated mostly by warez. RapidShare will charge your audience to download any file over 500 MB, or more than some limit per day. MegaUpload has an unspecified, "very limited" daily download limit. Both of these sites will make your audience wait (in some cases more than 2 minutes) unless they pay them, and MegaUpload uses popup ads. Using these sites may be free to you (in fact, anyone downloading your content gives points toward your free of charge premium membership or even cash rewards), but evidently your audience will be paying cash, since these companies are still around and thriving (and giving you cash). All in all, I don't see how anyone even remotely serious would use these services.

    Having said all this, I want to emphasize that the point of this post is how Alex presented the issue, not that I think what Google has done was good. Personally, I feel that it is a bit of a non-issue, since everyone knows how to spell "torrent" (and even if you don't, they will still auto-correct you, just not auto-complete).

    I normally like Alex as a host, both on TRS and Diggnation, and I sincerely hope he didn't intentionally spin this.

    Beer in a Can

    Actually, better beer (craft beer) has been making a huge impression in the canning world, and if you pay attention to what you are tasting, you will notice a much fresher, cleaner and more accurate representation of what the brewer intended the beer to taste like.

    Notice how green bottled beers have a musty taste to them? this is because light passes through a green bottle much easier and the beer becomes 'light struck', which 'skunks' the beer. Brown bottles alleviate the majority of this problem, and cans stop it entirely, 100%.

    Oskar Blues brewery out of Colorado (Dales Pale Ale) is 100% a canning operation (they were the first) and others are quickly starting to follow such as Breckenridge Brewery (Breck Avalanche), New Belgium (Fat Tire) and Big Sky (Trout Slayer).

    Cans are lighter weight to ship all over the US and cost a fraction of what bottles to do recycle.

    Great for the environment and better beer flavor overall, what more could you two clowns want?
